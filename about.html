About Austin

Thank you for visiting and for your attention.

For just shy of a decade, I have been designing a wide range of product experiences crossing many disciplines, industries, and teams. In 2014, I started among veterans on interactive experiences for higher education, bringing institutions into the modern era with responsive and mobile-first interfaces that brought clarity and ease of use to prospective students, faculty, and communities they serve. We won a few awards for our work including a MITX award for Educational Marketing.

Building onto my experience, I transitioned to product design where I quickly became in charge of the end-to-end mobile experience of Chewy.com's iOS app. Deeply integrated with the development team; I served as both the liaison between product teams and the advocate for the mobile experience, particularly focused on the Human Interface Guidelines and Accessibility. My goal within design is to always build for outcomes and to have fun with the little big details that add personality. To do this, I take great pride in my values and keeping an open mind when working with anyone.

Here is my approach

Firstly, it's not about me, it's about the person behind the glass.

The real person whom will use my work to buy the product they want, update their account information, chat with medical professionals, and determine whether this school is indeed right for them. I don't build things that don't benefit them or that do not keep them priority.

I allow people to make decisions and also change their mind.

I ask for feedback early and often, then relentlessly try new things.

It's best when I am in the room, but even better when everyone can contribute. I like to start with realistic, then go moonshot. I build trust, and work even harder to maintain it throughout.

In the past, I've told clients and stakeholders that my work isn't right, but with their help it will be the best foot forward to understanding the impact.

Start with off the shelf vs. bespoke.

Integrating myself with a development team gave me the insight into what is fast vs. what takes time. I've heard from a wise developer that anything is possible, but time (and money of course) is finite. By designing with what we have, I've been able to reach the right balance. From there, everything I do it additional to make that experience better or more familiar to the brand.

A traditional hamburger menu is not an an acceptable interface paradigm on an iOS app. No matter who tells you.

Handing off is not the end of the story.

Whenever possible, my goal is to help my projects come to life. I like working with members of the c-suite, engineers, content & copywriters, marketing, and QA throughout the life of the work. In recent times, adding in target 'users', researchers, and data scientists have really improved the outcomes of my design work. My best work comes when it's shared around.

I learn from mistakes, and push launched work further when and where it's right. I have a vast backlog of projects that I'd love to return to knowing what I know now.

I love to think and build in systems.

At iFactory and towards the end of my time there, I really dove head first into systems and building reusable components, styles, type, and more. Sketch really helped unlock a lot of power in my design and I optimize both the work and my time on building faster and accurately.

At Chewy, I worked with a small team of developers to build a better form than iOS could give us paying close attention to things like VoiceOver and introducing haptics.

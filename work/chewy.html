Universal App

For most of my time at Chewy I poured my attention, love, and energy behind our 'flagship' iOS and iPadOS app. We released a universal app based on a fresh new design I inherited, consolidated, and extended. In addition to our own priorities for the native mobile app, I also supported a wide variety of teams across Chewy in their initiatives around improving Autoship, introducing Gift Cards, as well as new business in Pharmacy and pet health.

iOS Design Systems

I built four evolving iterations of a design system for iOS with our native apps development team. At first, I built the system to consolidate the existing design that was being built for the universal app, but I quickly found use in using the system to accelerate time and design in high fidelity. Over the years, great considerations were made in terms of how we approach color for both light and dark modes, scaled the typography dynamically, created identifiable iconography, as well as highly-configurable and reusable components in Sketch.

A lot of considerations were made and a lot of collaboration occurred to build out really useful systems for our team. The last design system I built followed closely with the iOS design system provided by Apple. Unless locked down, every component was built to be customized.

eGift Cards

eGift Cards are Chewy's first and to my knowledge, only digital product. I had a starring role in the design, development, and release of this milestone. Over the course of 3 to 4 months, we built, iterated, presented, iterated, and shipped this feature to our customers. I worked on the iOS experience and assisted with future considerations that ultimately did not ship including a unified account balance.

Filter & Sort

Most of Chewy’s customers don't use the menu. They instead rely on searching; but with tens of thousands of products, I found multiple opportunities to help. I surfaced categories visually when browsing specific pet types like dog or reptile. In addition, we conducted user experience studies on a new filtrering mechanic which successfully gave customers insight into subcategories to find exactly what they are looking for.

Apple Pay

This is the feature that kept me at Chewy until launch and was one of the most customer-requested features. I was the lead designer and researcher on the feature both providing prototypes and documentation to product owners, developers, and leadership. This project spanned across iOS, mobile Safari, and desktop Safari browsers making it truly multi-platform. We even considered how it affects Android-based customers if they check thier order later on.

iMessage Stickers

A fun little project we worked on in the first year at Chewy. Taking illustrations originally created for Pet Profiles by David Blank, I created personified dog and cat emojis that are available to stick or send in iMessage and FaceTime calls and feature slight animation. The best part is that simply downloading the Chewy app gives you the stickers for free.
